Explore the history and beauty of the City of Lakes

History of Udaipur

Udaipur, known as the City of Lakes, was founded in 1553 by Maharana Udai Singh II. The city is renowned for its picturesque lakes, historic palaces, and vibrant culture. Udaipur's rich history is closely tied to the Mewar dynasty.

Famous Places to Visit

City Palace

The City Palace offers a splendid blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architecture, overlooking the serene waters of Lake Pichola.

Lake Pichola

Lake Pichola is a picturesque lake with two islands, Jag Niwas (Lake Palace) and Jag Mandir, providing stunning views and boat rides.

Saheliyon Ki Bari

Saheliyon Ki Bari is a beautiful garden adorned with fountains, lotus pools, marble pavilions, and lush greenery.

Monsoon Palace

The Monsoon Palace, also known as Sajjangarh Palace, offers panoramic views of Udaipur and its lakes, especially during the monsoon season.
