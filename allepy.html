Explore Alleppey, Kerala

Discover the serene backwaters and rich culture of Alleppey

History of Alleppey

Alleppey, often referred to as the "Venice of the East," has a rich history rooted in its significance as a trading port and hub for spice and coir industries. The region is known for its lush greenery, intricate network of backwaters, and traditional houseboat cruises.

Famous Places to Visit

Alleppey Backwaters

The backwaters of Alleppey are a mesmerizing labyrinth of canals, lagoons, and lakes, best explored through serene houseboat cruises.

Alappuzha Beach

Alappuzha Beach is a beautiful stretch of golden sands with a historic pier, offering stunning views and a tranquil atmosphere.

Nehru Trophy Snake Boat Race

The Nehru Trophy Snake Boat Race is a thrilling annual event that showcases the tradition and teamwork of snake boat racing on the Punnamada Lake.

Mullakkal Temple

Mullakkal Temple is a prominent Hindu temple known for its colorful architecture and religious significance.
