History of Munnar

Munnar, nestled in the Western Ghats, has a history closely tied to its tea plantation industry. The British colonial era witnessed the cultivation of tea in the region, resulting in the picturesque tea gardens that define Munnar's landscape today.

Famous Places to Visit

Tea Gardens

Munnar's tea gardens are a sight to behold, with lush green hills covered in neatly manicured tea plants that stretch as far as the eye can see.

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, Eravikulam National Park offers stunning landscapes, rolling hills, and diverse flora and fauna.

Mattupetty Dam

Mattupetty Dam is a popular attraction with its serene reservoir, lush surroundings, and opportunities for boating and picnicking.

Anamudi Peak

Anamudi Peak is the highest peak in South India, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.
