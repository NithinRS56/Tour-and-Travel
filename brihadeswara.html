Brihadeeswarar Temple

History of the Temple

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as the Big Temple, is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built during the Chola dynasty in the 11th century, it is a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Why You Should Visit

Visiting the Brihadeeswarar Temple offers a unique opportunity to marvel at the architectural brilliance of the Cholas. The temple's towering vimana, intricate sculptures, and historical significance make it a must-visit for history enthusiasts and spiritual seekers alike.

Nearby Places to Explore

Tanjore Royal Palace

The Tanjore Royal Palace, situated nearby, is an impressive complex showcasing the region's royal heritage and housing a museum with art, artifacts, and regal memorabilia.

Saraswathi Mahal Library

The Saraswathi Mahal Library is a treasure trove of ancient manuscripts, rare books, and historic documents, offering a glimpse into India's intellectual heritage.
